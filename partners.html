Welcome to the Protecting Water Mapper, where users can quickly and intuitively explore data and analyses that underpin The Nature Conservancy's global-scale reports, Beyond the Source: The Environmental, Economic and Community Benefits of Source Water Protection (forthcoming, January 2017), and Water Share: Using Water Markets and Impact Investment to Drive Sustainability (September 2016). This mapping site is part of a large and growing family of spatial decision tools supported through the Natural Solutions Toolkit.

Beyond the Source illustrates how nature-based solutions can help create a sustainable world and improve the lives of billions of people. From analysis of source watersheds of more than 4,000 large cities around the world, Beyond the Source finds that four out of five cities can improve water quality and quantity and provide a variety of other benefits through forest protection, reforestation and improved agricultural practices. The report highlights water funds as a successful mechanism for downstream water users to fund upstream land conservation and restoration, securing improved water quality and water supplies and other benefits in return.

Water Share explores the potential for water markets and impact investment to enhance global water scarcity. Today, nearly half of all people are affected by global water scarcity, and climate change will further exacerbate our water challenges. The report explores how water markets offer a powerful mechanism for alleviating scarcity, restoring ecosystems, and driving sustainable water management.

Protecting Water Atlas functions

Benefits Explorer: Investing in watersheds produces a range of benefits and contributes to addressing other social, economic and ecological challenges in addition to water security. The Benefits Explorer app allows users to see where investments may achieve the most co-benefits for people and nature.

Water Funds Explorer: Water funds are a source water protection mechanism for sharing the benefits of healthy watersheds across communities. The Water Funds Explorer app allows users to explore where water funds have been established, to learn about their key attributes, and to query across different water funds to learn the approaches and attributes various funds have in common.

Water Security Explorer (Coming Soon): Investing in watershed health is a sustainable way to help achieve water security. With the Water Security Explorer, a user will be able to identify (1) where sub-watersheds are of greatest importance for downstream drinking water, (2) where communities can reach user-defined water pollution reduction targets, and (3) what would be required to reach these targets in terms of area and cost.

Water Share Explorer (Coming Soon): Innovative investments can help address water depletion. The Water Share app enables users to look at how, where and when water has been used and explore potential strategies to alleviate scarcity within a given basin.
